# Reading List for Aditya Vikram Singh

A hand-curated list against the stated interest profile — cosmology, consciousness, computation, rationalism, biohacking/longevity, truth-seeking, family-as-anchor. Authors, titles, and themes verified to my knowledge; if any description sounds off, flag it.

---

## 1. Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid
- **Author:** Douglas Hofstadter
- **Why it fits:** The canonical bridge between computation, consciousness, and mathematical reasoning — built around the "strange loop" idea that minds are patterns made of patterns.
- **What to expect:** A demanding, rewarding tour de force linking Gödel's incompleteness, Escher's recursive prints, and Bach's self-referential fugues; reads best in chapters, not in one sitting.

## 2. The Beginning of Infinity: Explanations That Transform the World
- **Author:** David Deutsch
- **Why it fits:** A confident rationalist epistemology grounded in modern cosmology — knowledge as the fabric of reality, with the many-worlds interpretation as its backbone.
- **What to expect:** A physicist-philosopher's case that all problems are soluble in principle, ranging from epistemology to quantum theory to moral progress.

## 3. Lifespan: Why We Age — and Why We Don't Have To
- **Author:** David Sinclair
- **Why it fits:** The most-cited popular-science argument that aging is a treatable biological condition — squarely in the biohacking/longevity lane.
- **What to expect:** A Harvard geneticist's framework centered on sirtuins, NAD+/NMN, and partial reprogramming; read with a critical eye, then pair with skeptical sources (Attia's podcast skepticism, for one).

## 4. The Demon-Haunted World: Science as a Candle in the Dark
- **Author:** Carl Sagan
- **Why it fits:** The foundational popular toolkit for truth-seeking and disciplined skepticism, written with warmth rather than contempt.
- **What to expect:** Equal parts cosmic wonder and practical detection; the "baloney detection kit" chapter alone is worth the book.

## 5. Outlive: The Science and Art of Longevity
- **Author:** Peter Attia
- **Why it fits:** The current gold standard for an evidence-grounded, biohacking-adjacent longevity playbook — exercise, nutrition, sleep, emotional health — with explicit risk frameworks.
- **What to expect:** A clinical-style blueprint under Attia's "Medicine 3.0" framing; read for the operating framework, then stay disciplined about what is still unsettled.

## 6. The Elegant Universe: Superstrings, Hidden Dimensions, and the Quest for the Ultimate Theory
- **Author:** Brian Greene
- **Why it fits:** The most accessible tour of cosmology's unfinished masterpiece — string theory, hidden dimensions, M-theory — presented as honest physics rather than pop mysticism.
- **What to expect:** A careful, low-equation journey from Einstein to string theory with vivid analogies; lighter than a textbook, heavier than a magazine piece.

## 7. Consciousness Explained
- **Author:** Daniel Dennett
- **Why it fits:** A philosopher who treats consciousness as a computational phenomenon rather than a mystery — a rigorous rationalist counterweight to mystical takes.
- **What to expect:** A deflationary, multiple-drafts model of consciousness that demolishes the "Cartesian theater"; you'll either find it mind-expanding or infuriating, but you won't be bored.

## 8. Meditations
- **Author:** Marcus Aurelius (Gregory Hays translation recommended)
- **Why it fits:** A rationalist's private journal on attention, duty, and love — written by a family-anchored emperor who kept returning to the inner life despite running an empire.
- **What to expect:** Twelve short books of aphoristic self-instruction in a Stoic idiom; intimate in tone, surprisingly modern. Best read in small sittings.

---

### Suggested reading order

A natural pacing: start with *The Demon-Haunted World* and *Meditations* (short, grounding). Then *The Beginning of Infinity* and *Outlive* (operational plus epistemological). Finally *Gödel, Escher, Bach*, *Consciousness Explained*, *The Elegant Universe*, and *Lifespan* (deeper dives).
