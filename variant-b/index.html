I.

Now

I am a builder and a writer. I spent the early part of my career in venture capital, then operating roles at consumer hardware companies. I left to work on my own things. Most of what I work on is private. Some of it is not ready. Some of it may never be ready. I am at peace with that.

What I will say in public is the work I am most proud of, and the few things I am not willing to keep private.

The work I am proudest of right now is The Universe Is Not a Machine. It's a Mirror. — a three-part essay on what happens when you take the observer problem seriously. Cosmology, consciousness, and computation collapse into a single question. I think that question matters. The essay is my best attempt to ask it.

What I am not willing to keep private is the rest of my life. I have a young son. I have a partner who is patient with how I work. I have parents whose health I am actively trying to buy more of. I train most days. I take the boring protocols seriously: bloodwork, supplementation, sleep architecture, the unsexy compounding work. I read. I am wary of easy answers, including the ones inside my own tradition.

III.

A short shelf

A few books that did the work. Not the books I think I should recommend. The ones I keep coming back to.

  • On selfhood and pattern

    I Am a Strange Loop

    Douglas Hofstadter

    The self as pattern, not substance. Read it at 28. Wish I had read it at 19.

  • On knowledge and its edges

    The Beginning of Infinity

    David Deutsch

    Optimism as a position you earn with evidence. Not the same thing as feeling hopeful.

  • On what the body knows

    Outlive

    Peter Attia

    Longevity as a discipline of mechanism, not magic. A copy sits by my desk.

  • On what civilizations are for

    The Denial of Death

    Ernest Becker

    Why we build what we build.
